# 🧠 llm-wiki - Build your private automated knowledge base

llm-wiki acts as your dedicated personal assistant for information. It organizes your notes and documents into a structured knowledge base using artificial intelligence. This software follows the automated organization patterns popularized by Andrej Karpathy. The system reads your files, finds connections between topics, and links your ideas together without manual effort. You maintain full control over your data because everything stays on your local hard drive.

## 🛠️ System Requirements

- Operating System: Windows 10 or Windows 11.
- Processor: Any modern dual-core processor or better.
- Memory: 8 GB of RAM minimum. 16 GB recommended for large document collections.
- Storage Space: 500 MB for the core application, plus sufficient space for your notes.
- Internet: Required for initial setup and periodically to update language processing modules.

## 🚀 Setting Up Your Knowledge Base

Once the installation finishes, double-click the llm-wiki icon on your desktop to launch the program. The first time you open the software, it scans your computer for existing notes.

### Choose Your Source Folder

The program needs to know where you keep your written notes. Select a folder that contains your text files, markdown documents, or research notes. The software indexes these items to build your knowledge graph. You can change this location later through the settings menu.

### Configure Artificial Intelligence

The system utilizes large language models to categorize your notes. You need a connection to a processing service to enable these features. You can provide an API key from a provider like Anthropic or OpenAI. Enter the key into the provided box within the settings menu. This key allows the software to read your notes and extract meaningful connections between pages.

## 💎 Key Features

### Automated Linking
The system identifies related topics across your entire collection. It creates clickable links between notes so you can jump between concepts quickly.

### Intelligent Summaries
If you have a long document, the software generates a summary for you. This helps you grasp the main points of your own research without reading the full text.

### Obsidian Integration
Users of Obsidian find this tool very useful. It creates metadata that Obsidian recognizes automatically. This means your llm-wiki experience mirrors your Obsidian workflow perfectly.

### Privacy First
Your data never leaves your computer. The system sends only the text snippets necessary for analysis to the language model. Your files remain on your local drive at all times.

## 🔧 Frequently Asked Questions

### Does this work when I am offline?
You need an internet connection to process new notes. If you do not have internet, you can still view your notes, but the system cannot update the knowledge map.

### How much does this cost?
The software is free to use. You might pay a small fee to the company that provides the language processing service based on how much data you process. Many providers offer a free tier that works for most users.

### Can I delete the software?
Yes. Use the "Add or remove programs" feature in Windows settings to remove the application. Your notes and files remain safe in your folders.

### What files does the system support?
The software works best with `.txt` and `.md` files. It performs well with short notes and long research papers alike.

## 🩹 Troubleshooting

- Software does not start: Ensure your Windows version satisfies the requirement listed above. Restart your computer and try again.
- API key error: Check your key for extra spaces. Ensure your account with the provider has sufficient funds or credits.
- Missing links: Ensure the system has finished scanning your folder. You can see the progress bar at the bottom of the main window.
- High memory usage: The software uses your computer memory to process notes. If your computer feels slow, close other programs while the indexing process runs.

## 💡 Best Practices

- Organize your notes in logical folders.
- Use clear filenames.
- Apply consistent tags to your documents.
- Review the generated links every few days to ensure the connections make sense.
- Back up your notes folder separately to keep your information safe.
